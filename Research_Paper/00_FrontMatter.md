Abstract

The rapid proliferation of web and mobile applications has exponentially increased the attack surface for malicious actors, making crowd-sourced security models, commonly known as Bug Bounty programs, essential. However, the traditional Web2 bug bounty ecosystem is fundamentally plagued by trust deficits. Security researchers often complain of delayed payouts, ignored valid reports, and arbitrary reward downgrades by organizations. Conversely, organizations struggle with a massive influx of duplicate, low-quality, or plagiarized vulnerability reports that overwhelm their security triage teams. 

This project presents "Debug," a next-generation decentralized bug bounty platform that completely eliminates these trust and operational bottlenecks. Built entirely on Web3 architecture, the system employs Ethereum-based Smart Contracts to introduce a trustless Escrow mechanism. Organizations are required to lock bounty funds on-chain prior to publishing a program, ensuring that valid vulnerability disclosures mathematically guarantee a payout, thus protecting the researcher. To assist organizations, a Generative AI (LLM) Engine, integrated via the Google Gemini API, acts as an automated triage layer. Upon a researcher submitting a Proof of Concept (PoC), the AI instantaneously performs semantic plagiarism checks against historical databases, extracts the exploit mechanics, and assigns an objective severity score based on CVSS metrics.

By coupling the immutable financial guarantees of blockchain with the analytical speed of Large Language Models, "Debug" significantly reduces triage latency, prevents duplicate payouts, and establishes a mathematically verifiable trust layer between cybersecurity professionals and corporations.
