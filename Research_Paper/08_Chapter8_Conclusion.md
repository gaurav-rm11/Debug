Chapter 8: Conclusion

8.1 Limitations
Despite eliminating intermediary trust mechanics, "Debug" faces intrinsic adoption barriers:
1. Volatility of Gas Fees: Though significantly reduced through contract optimization (~40% efficiency gains), execution on primary Layer-1 (L1) chains is susceptible to rapid price surges depending on cumulative global network congestion.
2. AI Hallucination: The inherent boundary of Natural Language Processing models is the marginal risk of "hallucination," wherein the Gemini AI could artificially inflate the CVSS scoring logic derived from a highly convoluted semantic string, necessitating manual overview by corporate review vectors before final payout approval.
3. Cryptographic Adoption: The global cybersecurity workforce operates largely in conventional currency systems; transitioning independent researchers to maintain isolated private keys entails steep organizational curves.

8.2 Conclusion
"Debug" fundamentally subverts the outdated power structures innate to modern Bug Bounty operations. By establishing the "Bounty Escrow" Smart Contract as the singular axis of financial control, researchers are finally guaranteed algorithmic payouts free of corporate delays. Conversely, enterprises benefit dramatically by replacing hours of manual triage with millisecond responses from Google's Gemini GenAI models. The systematic elimination of duplicate submissions, combined with instantaneous semantic verification, radically drops organizational overhead. This project successfully establishes the cornerstone framework for a scalable, decentralized, and intelligent verification security landscape.

8.3 Future Scope
Future enhancements point toward implementing integration scaling protocols.
1. Multi-signature (Multi-Sig) Contract Modifications: Expanding the fundamental escrow state machine to allow decentralized arbitration where neutral experts can automatically break dispute gridlocks using chain-verifiable tokens.
2. Cross-Chain Protocol Deployments: Migrating the platform from purely EVM (Ethereum Virtual Machine) architectures onto hybrid Layer-0 networks to enable companies to fund bounties across isolated chains natively.
3. Automated Penetration Feedback Loop: Direct connection with Continuous Integration (CI/CD) pipelines, enabling the backend LLM not merely to triage exploits but subsequently compile dynamic patch configurations for instant developer integration tests.
